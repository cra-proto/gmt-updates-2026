Pay the tax

The tax payable in respect of a fiscal year must be paid on or before the GloBE Information Return (GIR) due date for the fiscal year.

On this page

  • How to pay the tax
  • Failure to pay the tax

How to pay the tax

There are two payment options available for paying the Global minimum tax: 

  • Wire transfers
  • Online payments through your bank or credit union starting May 11, 2026 (check availability with your financial institution) [reduced bolding]

Wire Transfers

Contact your bank or credit union to initiate the wire transfer.

Scotiabank processes wire transfers in Canada and does not charge any fee for forwarding your wire transfer to the CRA. 

You must make all wire transfers to the CRA in Canadian dollars.

You may be charged a fee by your financial institution for this service. Make sure that your financial institution does not deduct the wire transfer fee from the total payment amount due as this will result in an underpayment of tax.

Send your remittance details

After sending your wire transfer, provide your remittance details to the CRA's Revenue Processing Section by fax to 204 983 0924.

The remittance details must contain the following:

  • Name of the business making the payment
  • Global minimum tax program account (PT) number of the payor 
  • Name, position or office, telephone number and extension of the primary contact of the payor
  • Date of the payment
  • Amount of the payment 
  • Fiscal year to which the payment applies

If an appointed entity makes a payment on behalf of other members in the group, please include the following information for each member:

  • Business name  
  • PT program account number
  • Amount of the payment allocated 

For more information on how to make a wire transfer, refer to:  Pay at a bank or credit union through wire transfer.

Pay online with your bank or credit union

You can make a payment or schedule future payment(s) to the CRA directly by logging in to the online banking site or mobile banking app of most Canadian banks or credit unions. 

Check if your bank or credit union supports online payments to the CRA for businesses.  

Sign in to your bank or credit union’s tax filing service or online banking service for businesses. 

Make sure you select the correct option under “add a payee” so the CRA applies your payment correctly. 

Follow the instructions which would include entering your PT program account number. 

For more information on how to pay online with your bank or credit union, refer to:   Pay online with your bank or credit union.

Failure to pay the tax

If you fail to pay an amount, interest will apply from the day your payment was due. The interest rate we use is determined every three months, based on prescribed interest rates. Interest is compounded daily. 

For more information, refer to: Prescribed interest rates.

Filing due dates

In general, the following due dates will apply on an ongoing basis:

  • 18 months after the last day of the fiscal year, if it is the first fiscal year that an entity of the qualifying MNE group is subject to:
    • Part 3 of the Act
    • a qualified Income Inclusion Rule (IIR)
    • a qualified Undertaxed Profit Rule (UTPR)
  • 15 months after the last day of the fiscal year, in any other case

For any fiscal years beginning on or after December 31, 2023, and ending on or before December 31, 2024, the filing due date for the prescribed returns or notification is June 30, 2026.
