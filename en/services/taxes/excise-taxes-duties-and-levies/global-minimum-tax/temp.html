Administrative relief for 2024 GIR filings in another jurisdiction New

This temporary administrative relief applies only if your MNE group filed its 2024 GloBE Information Return (GIR) in another participating jurisdiction.

Canada follows the OECD common understanding on central GIR filing and exchange.

Learn more: Automatic exchange of GloBE Information Returns (GIR)

Although Canada has already activated several GIR exchange relationships, the CRA recognizes that other exchange relationships may not be activated by the GIR due date for the 2024 fiscal year. This may affect where some MNE groups are required to file a GIR.

Conditions for this relief

This temporary relief applies only if all of the following conditions are met:

  • the MNE group centrally filed a GIR in one of the participating jurisdictions on or before the applicable GIR due date, generally June 30, 2026
  • a GIR Notification was filed with the CRA on or before that date

For these cases, the GIR exchange deadline for the 2024 fiscal year is December 31, 2026.

When you do not need to file the GIR in Canada

The CRA will not require the MNE group to file the GIR locally in Canada if all of the following conditions are met:

  • the filing jurisdiction has a qualifying competent authority agreement with Canada that is in effect on or before the GIR exchange deadline
  • the CRA receives a complete or substantially complete GIR by the GIR exchange deadline

When you may still need to file the GIR in Canada

If the CRA has not received a complete or substantially complete GIR from the participating jurisdiction by the GIR exchange deadline, the GIR may still need to be filed in Canada.

If the MNE group has a qualifying foreign filing entity

The GIR must be filed in Canada within 30 days after the Minister notifies the relevant Canadian constituent entities or the designated notification entity that:

  • the CRA has not received the GIR
  • the GIR must be filed in Canada

If the MNE group does not have a qualifying foreign filing entity

The relevant Canadian constituent entities must file the GIR directly with the CRA promptly after the GIR exchange date if the filing jurisdiction does not have a qualifying competent authority agreement with Canada that is in effect on or before the GIR exchange deadline.
